hack:now Website and Branding
Duration: 1 month, Spring 2020
Team: Cal Hacks
Role: Lead brand ideation, collaborated with Jojo Chen and Julia Wang on the wesbite design and creation of marketing materials. Worked with Terrance Li and Noah Saso on building the website.
Context—
hack:now is an online hackathon for fostering solutions to challenges faced during the COVID-19 pandemic.
Hosted by Cal Hacks, the organizers of the world's largest colliegate hackathon, hack:now is a hackathon focused on tackling some of the most immediate problems we’re facing today. 2020 so far has been very eventful. Especially with the pandemic, it’s easy to feel like there’s little that just one individual can contribute, but that’s simply not true.
Purpose—
When brainstorming hackathon names, I realized that there may not be a better time to hack than... now.
Inspired by how current events shapes lives of society, we wanted to organize a hackathon for the student community to come up with solutions to current issues. Hosting it online not only meant adhering to social distancing standards but also making it more accessible to anyone, anywhere.

As the coronavirus continues to affect people in a variety of ways, ranging from health to communication to productivity, we wanted to leverage our reach.
Problem—
hack:now needed an identity that matched the purpose and goals of the hackathon.
hack:now is about meaningfully building solutions that make definite impact and we hope each iteration brings and unites people together during the times where we need to the most. Some key concepts the brand had to encompass:
  1. Humanity/Empathy
  2. Collaboration
  3. Community
Moodboard—
Envisioning collaboration and something "human."
Based on the values hack:now stood for, innovation and collaboration, I put together a moodboard to convey what I had in mind and worked with my design team on fonts and color scheme.

First Try—
hack:now's brand should feel more serious.
This concept was fun and friendly, but the elements that made it so also made it seem childish, which did not along with the seriousneness that comes with tackling current events.

Second Try—
Focus less on the news, more on the people.
This explored a "newspaper" theme since hack:now is about current events, but we felt like it lacked a friendly vibe.

Final Try—
The design of the website had to be attention grabbing and informative.
This website served as a place for people to go to for information and to eventually sign up! Below is the visual design we decided to go with.
